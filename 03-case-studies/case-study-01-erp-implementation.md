# Case Study 01 — $100M ERP Program Delivery
## Canadian Solar Inc. | IT Project Manager & Business Analyst

---

## Organization Profile

**Company:** Canadian Solar Inc.  
**Industry:** Renewable Energy / Solar Manufacturing  
**Scale:** $7B+ annual revenue; operations in 20+ countries  
**Employees:** 13,000+  
**Project Duration:** August 2016 – December 2020 (4.5 years)  
**Role:** IT Project Manager & Business Analyst  
**Portfolio Value:** $100M+ combined IT program

---

## Executive Summary

Led end-to-end delivery of a multi-year, multi-module enterprise ERP program across a global solar manufacturing organization. The program spanned Material Management, Human Resources, and Finance modules — touching operations, supply chain, HR, and finance functions across multiple international business units.

Delivered measurable improvements in data accuracy, operational efficiency, and on-time delivery while managing a cross-functional team of 25+ across IT, operations, finance, and external vendors in a fast-paced, high-volume manufacturing environment.

---

## The Challenge

Canadian Solar was experiencing significant operational growing pains as the business scaled rapidly across global markets:

**Operational Issues:**
- Disconnected legacy systems across business units — no single source of truth for inventory, HR, or financial data
- Manual procurement processes causing 3–5 day delays in material availability confirmation
- Inventory data accuracy at 71% — leading to overstock in some regions and stockouts in others
- Payroll processed through 6 separate regional systems with inconsistent data structures
- Financial consolidation required 12 business days of manual effort per quarter

**IT Issues:**
- No standardized integration layer between operational and financial systems
- Multiple parallel data entry points creating reconciliation burden
- Legacy ERP end-of-life with no vendor support path

**Business Impact:**
- Material shortages causing production line stoppages estimated at $2.4M annually
- Finance team spending 35% of effort on data reconciliation vs. value-added analysis
- Inability to produce consolidated global financial reports within reporting deadlines

---

## Approach

### Phase 1 — Discovery and Blueprint (Months 1–4)
- Conducted 40+ stakeholder interviews across operations, finance, HR, and IT in three countries
- Documented current-state processes using SIPOC and swimlane diagrams for 23 core business processes
- Identified 180+ functional requirements across the three ERP modules
- Produced Business Requirements Document (BRD) reviewed and signed off by 8 business owners
- Conducted vendor evaluation — assessed 3 ERP vendors against 47 weighted criteria
- Recommended SAP S/4HANA as the implementation platform; obtained executive approval

### Phase 2 — Design and Configuration (Months 5–12)
- Led 60+ design workshops translating business requirements into system configuration specifications
- Managed requirements traceability matrix (RTM) tracking 180 requirements through design, build, and test
- Coordinated solution architecture across Material Management, HR, and Finance workstreams
- Managed vendor delivery against contractual milestones — bi-weekly governance cadence
- Led change impact assessment identifying 340 process changes across 12 departments

### Phase 3 — Build and Test (Months 13–22)
- Oversaw system integration testing (SIT) across 3 ERP modules and 7 legacy system integrations
- Led business-side UAT for all three modules — 280 test scenarios executed
- Managed defect triage — 94 defects identified; 91 resolved before go-live; 3 accepted with workaround
- Coordinated parallel run — 6-week period of running old and new systems simultaneously
- Delivered training program — 340 end users trained across 3 countries in 4 weeks

### Phase 4 — Go-Live and Stabilization (Months 23–30)
- Managed phased go-live across 3 business units in 3 countries over 90 days
- Led 8-week hypercare period post go-live — daily war room, issue triage, and resolution
- Transitioned to BAU support with documented runbooks and handover packages

## Key Challenges and How They Were Managed

**Challenge 1: Scope creep across 25 stakeholders**
With 25+ stakeholders across multiple countries and business units, scope change requests were constant. Implemented a formal change control process — all requests required a written change request with impact assessment and sponsor approval. Rejected or deferred 23 of 31 change requests in Year 1, protecting budget and timeline.

**Challenge 2: Cross-cultural stakeholder management**
Teams in Canada, China, and Europe had different communication styles, decision-making norms, and availability windows. Established a structured governance model with documented decision rights, asynchronous status updates, and regional business leads as single points of contact for each geography.

**Challenge 3: Vendor delivery delays in Year 2**
The primary implementation vendor fell 6 weeks behind schedule on the HR module configuration. Invoked contract milestone escalation clause, required a remediation plan within 5 business days, and brought in an additional 3 vendor resources. Schedule was recovered within 4 weeks with no impact to the go-live date.

**Challenge 4: Data migration quality**
Historical data migration from 6 legacy systems revealed significant data quality issues — 18% of records had missing or inconsistent values. Led a 10-week data cleansing effort in parallel with system build, prioritizing the 6 critical data objects needed for go-live. Migration reconciliation report confirmed 99.2% record integrity at go-live.

---

## Lessons Learned

1. **Requirements sign-off must be formal and documented** — Verbal agreement is not agreement. Every requirement change must be in writing with a date and name.
2. **Change control saves projects** — The formal change control process rejected 74% of change requests, directly preventing scope and budget overrun.
3. **Data quality is a project in itself** — Treat data migration as a parallel workstream with its own PM, timeline, and quality gates — not a task in the build workstream.
4. **Phased go-live reduces risk** — Rolling go-live across business units allowed lessons from the first unit to improve delivery for subsequent units.
5. **Hypercare period must be fully resourced** — Under-resourcing the post-go-live support period is the most common cause of ERP adoption failure.
